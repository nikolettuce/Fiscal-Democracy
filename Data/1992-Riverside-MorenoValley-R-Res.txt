RESOLUTION NO. 92-5 9

A RESOLUTION OF THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF
MORENO VALLEY, CALIFORNIA, CALLING AND GIVING
NOTICE OF THE HOLDING OF A SPECIAL MUNICIPAL
ELECTION ON THE ESTABLISHMENT OF A GRAFFITI
PREVENTION TAX

WHEREAS, Chapter 3 of Part 1.7 of Division 2 of the California Revenue and Taxation
Code authorizes cities to levy a tax by ordinance, approved by a 2/3 vote of the electors, at
specified rates on the sale at retail within its jurisdiction of aerosol paint containers, containers
of any other marking substance, felt tip markers with a specified writing surface, and any other
marking instrument; and

WHEREAS, said Chapter requires the State Board of Equalization to allocate the tax
revenues, as specified, and requires each local entity to expend those revenues for purposes
of the removal and prevention of graffiti or for educational programs for at-risk youth to prevent
graffiti vandalism.

NOW, THEREFORE, THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF MORENO VALLEY,
CALIFORNIA, DOES HEREBY RESOLVE, DECLARE, DETERMINE AND ORDER AS FOLLOWS:

SECTION 1. A special election is called and ordered and will be held in the City of
Moreno Valley on November 3, 1992 for the purpose of submitting to the qualified voters of
the City the following question:

“Shall the City Council of the City of Moreno Valley be authorized to levy a graffiti	YES	
prevention tax on the sale at retail of aerosol paint containers, containers of any other		
marking substance, certain felt tip markers or any other marking instruments for a period of five years in the amount as specified in proposed Ordinance No. 341?"	NO	

SECTION 2. If the measure receives approval by a 2/3 vote of the electors, the
measure is adopted and will become effective on the first day of the month following the
month in which the City contracts with the State Board of Equalization to perform all functions
incident to the administration of Ordinance No. 341.

SECTION 3. The election shall be held and conducted, the votes canvassed, the
returns thereof made, and result determined as provided by law in all particulars as are other
municipal elections.

SECTION 4. That the polls for the election shall be open at seven o'clock a.m. of the
day of the election and shall remain open continuously from that time until eight o'clock p.m.
of the same day when the polls shall be closed, except as provided in Section 14301 of the
Elections Code of the State of California.

SECTION 5. That notice of the time and place of holding the election is given and the
City Clerk is authorized, instructed and directed to give further or additional notice of the
election, in time, form and manner as required by law.

SECTION 6. That an impartial analysis, not to exceed five hundred (500) words in
length of the measure showing the effect of the measure on the existing law and the operation
of the measure, be prepared by the City Attorney as set forth in Section 5011 of the California
Elections code.

SECTION 7. (a) Notice is hereby given that written arguments not to exceed three
hundred (300) words in length, shall be filed not later than 5:00 p.m. on August 17, 1992 at
the office of the City Clerk, Building B, 23119 Cottonwood Avenue, Moreno Valley, California.

(b)	Each argument submitted for or against approval of the foregoing
ordinance shall be accompanied by the statement set forth in Section 5350 of the California
Elections Code, must be completed and signed by the author of such argument, and shall
comply with the provisions of Section 5010 through 5016 of the California Elections Code.

(c)	A proclamation of a special election is hereby issued in accordance
with the terms and conditions hereinabove stated.

SECTION 8. That the City Clerk shall certify to the passage and adoption of this
Resolution and enter it into the book of original Resolutions.

APPROVED AS TO FORM:

Edward Duddy, City Attorney
