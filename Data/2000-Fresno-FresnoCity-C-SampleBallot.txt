CITY OF FRESNO
TRANSIENT ROOM TAX (HOTEL ROOM TAX)

MEASURE C

FULL TEXT OF MEASURE

Shall the City of Fresno increase the transient room tax (hotel room tax) from 12% to 13% for persons
staying in hotels?

IMPARTIAL ANALYSIS BY CITY ATTORNEY

The Transient Room Tax Ordinance of the City of Fresno was adopted in 1964. Under this Ordinance,
persons staying in hotels for 30 consecutive calendar days or less, are required to pay a transient room
tax. The transient room tax, also known as the hotel room tax, are collected by the operators of the
hotels and deposited into the City's General Fund.

The current hotel room tax rate is 12% of the rent charged for a hotel room. On June 27,2000, the City
Council adopted a 1% increase in the hotel room tax in Resolution No. 2000-201. Under Proposition
218 (Article XIIIC, Section 2 of the California Constitution), any increase in the general tax must be
submitted for voter approval. Voters must approve the 1% increase in the hotel room tax for it to
become effective. For this reason, the City Council placed this Measure on the ballot.

The Measure requires a simple majority vote to pass. If the Measure passes, the hotel room tax will be
increased to 13%. If the Measure fails, the hotel room tax will remain at 12%. The City Controller
advises that the 1% increase to the hotel room tax will generate approximately $500,000 annually to the
City’s General Fund. A “yes” vote is a vote approving the 1% increase in the hotel room tax. A “no” vote
is a vote against the 1% rate increase in the hotel room tax.

s/ Hilda Cantu Montoy
City Attorney

ARGUMENT AGAINST

Now that Fresno’s new convention center is attracting more and bigger conventions to the city, the last
thing we should be doing is making it more expensive for convention attendees to stay in our city.

Fresno’s current 12% tax rate is already one of the highest in California. Only Los Angeles, San
Francisco, and Anaheim have higher rates. It is unlikely Fresno could compete with those cities for
convention business anyway. Of the cities that do compete with Fresno, Sacramento and Bakersfield
both have 12% hotel tax rates. Visalia’s rate is only 10%, Modesto and Oakland have 9% rates, and
Stockton’s is only 8%. Rather than raising our rate, Fresno should lower its rate to be more competitive.

In addition to reducing Fresno’s attractiveness as a convention destination, raising our hotel tax rate will
hurt other local businesses. Every dollar that visitors have to turn over to the government is a dollar that
they won’t have to spend at local clubs, restaurants and other area businesses. Fresno already has a
hotel vacancy rate that is one of the highest in the state. Does anybody really believe that making hotel
stays more expensive will solve that problem? If Fresno wants to be a tourist attraction, it needs to make
it easier for visitors to afford to come here.

The tax proponents say that this is an easy way to raise money from people who don’t live here. Think
about that the next time your family or friends come here to visit. Think about it when the Fresno State
football team is turned down in their efforts to get into a more competitive conference. And think about
it when a convention that might have come to Fresno, goes to Visalia or Modesto instead.

s/ Jonathan Richter
Libertarian Party of Fresno County
