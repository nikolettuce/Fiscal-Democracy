RESOLUTION NO. 90™	4

A RESOLUTION OF THE TOWN COUNCIL OF THE TOWN OF PARADISE r
CALIFORNIA, REQUESTING THE BOARD OF SUPERVISORS OF THE

COUNTY OF BUTTE TO CONSOLIDATE A SPECIAL MUNICIPAL
ELECTION TO BE HELD ON JUNE 5, 1990, WITH THE
STATEWIDE PRIMARY ELECTION TO BE HELD ON THE
DATE PURSUANT TO SECTION 23302 OF THE ELECTIONS CODE

WHEREAS, the Town Council of the Town of Paradise desires
to submit an ''Advisory" measure to the voters relating to public
safety services in the Town of Paradise; and

WHEREAS, it is desirable that the Special Municipal
Election be consolidated with the Statewide Primary Election to be
held on the same date and that within the Town the precincts,
polling places and election officers of the two elections be the
same, and that the County Election Department of the County of
Butte canvass the returns of the Special Municipal Election and
that the elections be held in all respects as if there were only
one election;

NOW, THEREFORE, the Town Council of the Town of Paradise
does resolve, declare, determine and order as follows;

SECTION 1. That an "Advisory" measure is to appear on
the ballot as follows:

ADVISORY VOTE ONLY		
Shall the citizens of Paradise	YES	
impose a 4% utility tax in order		
to maintain the current level of		
public safety services?	NO	

SECTION 2. That the County Election Department is
authorized to canvass the returns of the Special Municipal
Election. The election shall be held in all respects as if there
were only one election, and only one form of ballot shall be used.

SECTION 3. That the Board of Supervisors is requested
to issue instructions to the County Election Department to take any
and al 1 steps necessary for the holding of the consolidated
election.

SECTION 4. That the Town of Paradise recognizes that
additional costs will be incurred by the County by reason of this
consolidation and agrees to reimburse the County for any costs.
