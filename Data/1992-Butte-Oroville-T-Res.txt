CITY OF OROVILLE

RESOLUTION NO. 4721

A RESOLUTION DIRECTING THE CITY CLERK TO SUBMIT TO THE VOTERS AT
THE GENERAL MUNICIPAL ELECTION A QUESTION CONCERNING AN AMENDMENT
)F SECTION 24-104 OF THE OROVILLE CITY CODE TO INCREASE THE RATE OF
TRANSIENT OCCUPANCY TAX FROM SIX (6%) PERCENT TO NINE (9%) PERCENT

WHEREAS, The Oroville Area Chamber of Commerce has requested
that in order to help stimulate the local economy and to
provide additional funds for much needed City services, the
rate of transient occupancy tax be raised from SIX (6%)
percent to NINE (9%) percent, and

WHEREAS, it is in the best interests of the City of Oroville
that the question be submitted to the voters of the City of
Oroville at the next General Municipal Election,

NOW, THEREFORE, be it hereby RESOLVED by the Oroville City

Council as follows:

SECTION 1:	The	City	Clerk	is directed to place on the ballot

at the General Municipal Election scheduled for
Tuesday, November 3, 1992, the following question:

"Shall section 24-104 of the Oroville City Code be
amended to read as follows:

"Sec. 24-104. Amount; where and when payable.

(a)	For the privilege of occupancy in any
hotel, each transient is subject to and shall pay
a tax in the amount of nine percent of the rent
charged by the operator. Such tax constitutes a debt
owed by the transient to the city, which is
extinguished only by payment to the operator or to
the city. The transient shall pay the tax to the
operator of the hotel at the time the rent is paid.
If the rent is paid in installments, a proportionate
share of the tax shall be paid with each
installment. The unpaid tax shall be due upon the
transient ceasing to occupy space in the hotel. If
for any reason the tax due is not paid to the
operator of the hotel, payment of such tax shall be
made directly to the director of finance.

(b)	All taxes collected pursuant to this
section shall be allocated as follows:
