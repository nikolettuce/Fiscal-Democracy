Measure A:

Section 14-603 of the Burbank Municipal Code is hereby amended to read as follows:

“Sec. 14-603. Tax Imposed.

“For the privilege of occupancy of any hotel accommodation, each transient is subject to and shall pay a tax in an amount not
exceeding twelve percent (12%) of the rent. The actual tax rate imposed shall be established, and from time to time may be
modified, by resolution of the City Council, after a public hearing on such matter, provided, however, that the rate as so established
or modified shall not exceed twelve percent (12%) of the rent. This tax shall constitute a debt owed by the transient to the City
which is extinguished only by payment to the operator of the hotel at the time the rent is paid. If the rent is paid in installments,
a proportionate share of the tax shall be paid with each installment. The unpaid tax shall be due upon the transient’s ceasing to
occupy space in the hotel. If for any reason the tax due is not paid to the operator of the hotel, the Director may require that such
tax shall be paid directly to the License Division.”

IMPARTIAL ANALYSIS OF MEASURE A

The City of Burbank’s Transient Occupancy Tax is imposed on persons who pay for short-term accommodations at a hotel, motel or
other lodging facility in the City. The tax is collected by the facility’s operator from each guest at the time the guest’s bill is paid and
then remitted to the City. The tax proceeds are deposited into the City’s General Fund and are used to pay for general City services
such as police and fire protection, parks, recreation programs, libraries, street maintenance, and traffic control.

The tax generally does not apply to anyone who resides in a hotel, motel or other lodging facility for longer than thirty days. It does
not apply to other services typically offered at hotels and motels, such as food and beverage service, banquet rooms or conference
facilities.

The City’s municipal code provides that the tax rate of the Transient Occupancy Tax shall be set by the City Council in the City’s
annual fee resolution. The current tax rate is 10% of the room rental charges paid by the guest to the facility operator. This is the
same rate that has been in effect since 1983.

This measure would amend section 14-603 of the Burbank Municipal Code to allow the City Council, after holding a public hearing,
to increase the tax to a maximum rate of 12% of room rental charges. If the measure passes, the City Council could adjust the rate
from time to time but could never raise it above 12% without additional voter approval. The measure does not affect any other taxes,
including property taxes, paid by Burbank residents and property owners.

If a majority of votes cast on this ballot measure are “Yes,” this measure will be approved and the City Council would then have the
authority to increase the City’s Transient Occupancy Tax to a maximum rate of 12%. If a majority of votes are “No,” this measure
will not be approved and the transient occupancy tax will continue to be collected at the current rate of 10%.

ANALISIS IMPARCIAL DE LA INICIATIVA DE LEYA

El Impuesto a la Ocupacion Transitoria de la Ciudad de Burbank se aplica a personas que pagan una estadfa breve en un hotel,
motel u otra instalacibn de hospedaje en la Ciudad. Al impuesto lo recauda el operador de (a instalacion por cada huesped al
momento del pago de la cuenta y luego es remitido a la Ciudad. El producto del impuesto se deposita en el Fondo General de la
Ciudad y es utilizado para pagar fos servicios generales de la Ciudad como proteccibn de policfa y bomberos, parques, programas
de recreacion, bibliotecas, mantenimiento de calles y control del trbnsito.

El impuesto, generalmente, no se aplica a las personas que residen en un hotel, motel u otra instalacibn de hospedaje por mas
de treinta dias. Tampoco se aplica a otros servicios tfpicamente ofrecidos en hoteles y moteles como ser servicios de alimentos y
bebidas, salas para banquetes o instalaciones para conferencias.

El codigo municipal de la Ciudad establece que la tasa del Impuesto a la Ocupacion Transitoria deberb ser fijada por el Concejo
Municipal en la resolucion tarifaria anual de la Ciudad. La tasa actual del impuesto es del 10% de los cargos de renta de habitacion
pagados por el huesped al operador de la instalacibn. Esta es la misma tasa que ha estado vigente desae 1983.

habitacion. Si la iniciativa de ley es aprobada, el Concejo Municipal podrfa ajustar la tasa de vez en cuando pero nunca podrfa
aumentarla por encima de 12% sin la aprobacion de los votantes. La iniciativa de ley no afecta otros impuestos, incluyendo los
impuestos a la propiedad pagados por los residentes y propietarios de casa de Burbank.

Si la mayoria de los votos emitidos por esta iniciativa de ley son “A Favor”, la misma sera aprobada y el Concejo Municipal tendra
entonces la facultad para aumentar el Impuesto a la Ocupacion Transitoria de la Ciudad a una tasa maxima de 12%. Si fa mayoria
