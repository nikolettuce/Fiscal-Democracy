TITLE, SUMMARY AND IMPARTIAL ANALYSIS OF
CITY OF WHEATLAND TRANSACTIONS AND USETAX MEASURE

Ballot Title: City of Wheatland Transactions and Use Tax (aka sales
tax increase)

The Wheatland City Council proposes to adopt a transactions and
use tax. The City Council approved the tax measure, subject to approval
by a majority of the City voters.

The transactions and use tax would be charged to City retailers
at the time of sale of any goods and on the storage, use or consumption
of goods within the City. The tax would be collected by the State
Board of Equalization at the same time and in the same manner that
the Board collects the ordinary state sales taxes from City retailers.
The Board would return the tax revenue to the City The City would
deposit the revenue in its general fund and use the revenue to fund
general City services (for example, Police Department, Fire
Department, parks and recreation).

The amount of the tax would be one-half of one percent (0.5%),
which is a tax of one cent for every two dollars of goods sold within
the City. If the voters approve the transactions and use tax measure,
it would effectively raise the sales tax that is charged on purchases
of goods within the City by one-half of one percent. The transactions
and use tax would only be collected for ten years, unless the voters
approve another measure to extend the tax.

Under state law, the voters of the City must approve a new City
tax. Accordingly, the City Council is requesting the voters to approve
this tax. It is a general tax and requires majority voter approval.

A “Yes” vote on the measure would be a vote to approve the tax.

A “No” vote on the measure would be a vote against the tax.

Tftulo de la Balota: Impuesto sobre Transacciones y Uso de la
Ciudad de Wheatland (tambien conocido como aumento de
impuesto a las ventas)

El Concejo Municipal de la Ciudad de Wheatland propone adoptar
un impuesto sobre transacciones y uso. El Concejo Municipal aprobo
la medida sobre el impuesto, sujeta a la aprobacion de una mayoria
de votantes de la ciudad.

El impuesto sobre transacciones y uso serfa cobrado a los
comerciantes en la ciudad en el momento de venta de cualquier
producto, y el almanaceje de, uso o consumo de mercancia dentro
de la ciudad. El impuesto serfa recaudado por la Directiva Estatal
de Impuestos a la misma vez y de la misma manera que cobra los
impuestos normales sobre la venta de los comerciantes en la ciudad.
La Directiva regresarfa las rentas del impuesto a la ciudad. La ciudad
depositarfa los ingresos en su fondo general y los usarfa para pagar
servicios generates de la ciudad (por ejemplo, del Departamento de
Policfa, el Departamento de Bomberos, Parques y Recreacion).

El monto del impuesto serfa medio punto porcentual (0.5%), es
decir, un impuesto de un centavo por cada dos dolares de bienes
vendidos dentro de la ciudad. Si los votantes aprueban la medida
sobre el impuesto sobre transacciones y uso, tendrfa el efecto de
aumentar el impuesto sobre las ventas que se cobra en la compra
de bienes dentro de la ciudad por la mitad del uno por ciento. El
impuesto sobre transacciones y uso serfa cobrado solamente durante
diez anos, a menos que los votantes aprobaran otra medida para
extender el impuesto.

Segun la ley del estado, los votantes de la ciudad tendrfan que
aprobar un impuesto nuevo en la ciudad. Por eso, el City Council
pide que los votantes aprueben ese impuesto. Es un impuesto general
y require la aprobacion de la mayoria de los votantes.

Un voto de “Sf” en la medida serfa un voto para aprobar el impuesto.

Un voto de “No” en la medida serfa un voto en contra del impuesto.

ARGUMENT IN FAVOR OF MEASURE S

Keep the City of Wheatland a great place to live! Vote YES on Measure
S Protect our neighborhood parks, police and fire departments. Without
the revenue produced by Measure S our emergency services could
be further reduced.

Wheatland has suffered from a decrease in revenues during the past
three years while city positions have been eliminated; our city
employees continue to take non-paid furlough days and all city budgets
have been reduced.

The City has been diligent about controlling expenses, we face deficits
that additional budget cutting can’t solve.

During the past two years revenue from the City’s property tax has
been reduced by $307,000!

The state has continued to raid the funds of local governments including
Wheatland to finance the state budget.

This initiative will add one half percent to the sales tax; it will produce
approximately $50,000 in yearly revenue that cannot be shared by
the state. Non-Wheatland residents will generate incomes for us as
they make purchases in town.

Measure S is a responsible and conservative request for funding to
protect our services and maintain our property values. Economic times
like these require us to come together and protect the quality of our
community.

This Measure is endorsed by a diverse group of Wheatland residents.

Please vote YES on Measure S, help keep Wheatland a safe place
to live.
