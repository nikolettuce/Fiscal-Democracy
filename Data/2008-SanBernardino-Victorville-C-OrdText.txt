RESOLUTION NO. 08-125

A RESOLUTION OF THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF VICTORVILLE, COUNTY OF SAN
BERNARDINO, STATE OF CALIFORNIA, ORDERING THE SUBMISSION TO THE QUALIFIED
ELECTORS OF THE CITY A CERTAIN MEASURE RELATING TO THE TRANSIENT OCCUPANCY
TAX (TOT) RATE; CALLING AND GIVING NOTICE OF THE HOLDING OF A SPECIAL ELECTION
IN SAID CITY TO BE HELD ON TUESDAY, NOVEMBER 4, 2008, AND CONSOLIDATED WITH
THE STATEWIDE GENERAL ELECTION; REQUESTING THE BOARD OF SUPERVISORS OF
SAN BERNARDINO COUNTY TO PERMIT THE REGISTRAR OF VOTERS OFFICE TO
CONDUCT SAID SPECIAL ELECTION; AND SETTING THE DATE AFTER WHICH NO
ARGUMENTS FOR OR AGAINST THE QUESTION TO SAID ARGUMENTS MAY BE
SUBMITTED TO THE CITY CLERK, AND SUBMITTING THE BALLOT MEASURE TO THE CITY

ATTORNEY FOR AN IMPARTIAL ANALYSIS

WHEREAS, under the provisions of the law, a Special Municipal Election (the "Election")
shall be held on November 4, 2008 for the purpose of submitting to the voters a ballot proposal
relating to an increase in the Transient Occupancy Tax (TOT) rate from seven percent (7%) to
ten percent (10%); and

WHEREAS, it is desirable that said Election be consolidated with the Statewide General
Election to be held on November 4, 2008; that within the City the precincts, polling places and
election officers of the two elections be the same; that the Registrar of Voters of the County of
San Bernardino canvass the returns of the Election; and that said Election and Statewide
General Election be held in all respects as if they were one.

NOW, THEREFORE, THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF VICTORVILLE DOES HEREBY RESOLVE
AS FOLLOWS:

SECTION 1. That the foregoing recitals and true and correct.

SECTION 2. That the City Council of the City of Victorville does hereby call and give
notice of an Election to be held on November 4, 2008 within the boundaries of the City.

SECTION 3. That the City Council of the City of Victorville, pursuant to its right and
authority to do so, does hereby determine that there shall be and there is hereby ordered
submitted to the qualified electors of the City at the Election the following question:

CITY MEASURE "_____INCREASE IN TRANSIENT OCCUPANCY TAX RATE

Shall Resolution No. 08-130 of the City of Victorville be adopted,

increasing the Transient Occupancy Tax (hotel/motel tax) from	YES	____

seven percent (7%) to ten percent (10%), which is imposed upon	NO	____

transients, to fund general City services?

SECTION 4. That the text of the Resolution submitted to the voters is attached as
"Exhibit A - Resolution No. 08-130."

SECTION 5. In the event that the increase in the Transient Occupancy Tax rate from
seven (7%) percent to ten percent (10%) is approved by a majority vote of the electors casting
ballots on November 4, 2008, the increased tax shall be effective on January 1, 2009, so long as
the City has received certification of the election from the County of San Bernardino Registrar
of Voters.

Section 6. In the event that the increase in the Transient Occupancy Tax rate from
seven (7%) percent to ten percent (10%) is approved by a majority vote of the electors casting
ballots on November 4, 2008, the increased tax shall be collected in the same manner as the
rate currently imposed, as set forth in Victorville Municipal Code Chapter 3.12 (Transient
Occupancy Tax).

SECTION 7. In the event that the increase in the Transient Occupancy Tax rate from
seven (7%) percent to ten (10%) percent is not approved by a majority vote of the electors
casting ballots on November 4, 2008, there shall be no increase in said tax rate.

SECTION 8. That notice of the time and place of holding the election is given and the
City Clerk is authorized, instructed and directed to give further or additional notice of the
election, in time, form and manner as required by law.

SECTION	9.	Any and all members of the	Victorville City Council, any individual voter

who	is eligible	to	vote on the measure, or a	bona fide association of citizens, or any

combination of voters and associations, are authorized to file a written argument(s) regarding
the City measure as specified above, accompanied by the printed name(s) and signature(s) of
the author(s) submitting it, in accordance with Article 4, Chapter 3, Division 9 of the Elections
Code of the State of California and are authorized to change the argument until and including
the date fixed by the Registrar of Voters after which no arguments for or against the City
measure may be submitted to the City Clerk.

SECTION	10.	Pursuant to Section 9280 of	the Elections Code of the State of California,

the City Council	directs the City Clerk to transmit a	copy of the ballot measure to the Victorville

City Attorney. The City Attorney shall prepare an impartial analysis of the ballot measure, not
to exceed five hundred (500) words in length, showing the effect of the measure on existing law
and the operation of the measure. The impartial analysis shall be filed by the date set by the
Registrar of Voters for the filing of primary arguments.

SECTION 11. That pursuant to the requirements of the Elections Code of the State of
California, the Board of Supervisors of the County of San Bernardino is hereby requested to
consent and agree to the consolidation of such Election with the General Election to be held on
November 4, 2008. The Board of Supervisors is hereby requested to issue instructions to the

Registrar of Voters to take any and all steps necessary for the holding of the consolidated
election.

SECTION 12. The Registrar of Voters of the County of San Bernardino is hereby
authorized to procure and furnish any and all official ballots, notices, printed matter and all
supplies, equipment and paraphernalia that may be necessary in order to properly and lawfully
conduct the election. The ballots to be used at the election shall be, both as to form and
matter contained therein, such as may be required by law to be used at such election.

SECTION 13. That the City Clerk of the City of Victorville is hereby directed to
immediately file a certified copy of this resolution with the Board of Supervisors and the
Registrar of Voters of the County of San Bernardino, and to enter it into the book of original
resolutions.

EXHIBIT "A"
MEASURE"

RESOLUTION NO. 08-130

A RESOLUTION OF THE CITY OF VICTORVILLE, CALIFORNIA, INCREASING THE TRANSIENT
OCCUPANCY TAX (TOT) RATE FROM SEVEN PERCENT (7%) TO TEN PERCENT (10%)

THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF VICTORVILLE DOES RESOLVE AS FOLLOWS:

Section 1. Chapter 3.12 of the Victorville Municipal Code governs the local law on
Transient Occupancy Tax. Section 3.12.030 provides that the amount of the Transient
Occupancy Tax shall be in an amount as set forth by Resolution of the City Council.

Section 2. The City Council hereby adopts this Resolution to increase the Transient
Occupancy Tax (TOT) rate from seven percent (7%) to ten percent (10%). The increase shall
take effect January 1, 2009, upon approval by the voters of the City of Victorville and
certification of the election by the County of San Bernardino Registrar of Voters.

Section 3. In the event that the increase in the Transient Occupancy Tax rate from
seven (7%) percent to ten percent (10%) is approved by a majority vote of the electors casting
ballots on November 4, 2008, the increased tax shall be collected in the same manner as the
rate currently imposed, as set forth in Victorville Municipal Code Chapter 3.12 (Transient
Occupancy Tax).

Section 4. If any provision of this Resolution or the application thereof to any
person or property is held invalid or unconstitutional by any court of competent jurisdiction,
such invalidity or unconstitutionality shall not affect any other provision or applications, and to
this end, the provisions of this Resolution are declared to be severable. The City Council hereby
declares that it would have adopted this Resolution and each section, subsection, sentence,
clause, phrase, part or portion thereof, irrespective of the fact that any one or more sections,
subsections, sentences, clauses, phrases, parts or portions thereof, be declared invalid or
unconstitutional.

Section 5. This Resolution or any provision hereof, if any which provides for
modification by the City Council of the City of Victorville, may only be amended or repealed by
approval of a majority (51%) of the voters voting on the provisions at an election.

Section 6. This Resolution shall be effective only if approved by a majority of the
voters voting at a general election to be held on November 4, 2008, and shall take effect on
January 1, 2009, after the Registrar of Voters of the County of San Bernardino has certified that
this Resolution was approved by a majority of the voters voting thereon.

PASSED, APPROVED AND ADOPTED this 5th day of AUGUST 2008.
