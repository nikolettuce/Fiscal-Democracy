IMPARTIAL ANALYSIS BY COUNTY COUNSEL ,

: ;r:; ‘. MEASURE D

•A “Yes” vote by two-thirds of the voters voting on this measure will
authorize the Mojave Unified School District to levy a special tax for a period
of two (2) years commencing July 1,1989. This special tax will be imposed
on ail parcels of land located in the district. The proceeds of this tax will be
used only for the purposes of school site acquisition and improvement,
school "facility construction, and acquisition and installation of furniture and
equipment. S.'f:?'	'V'H	•

The proposed special tax is authorized by Article XIII A of the California
Constitution (Prop. 13) and Sections 50075-50077 and 50079 of the Califor-
nia Government Code. These provisions require approval by two-thirds of the
voters voting pn this measure before the special tax can.be levied.

The District Intends to. use the proceeds from the special tax to construct
arid furnish-a, new mjddle school in California City and to construct additional
school facilities at Joshua Middle School in Mojave,, including additional
classrooms, restrooms and a gymnasium.:	'*

- Approval ^ this measure will .authorize the levy, of an additional tax of
$55.35 par year per parcel for a period of two (2), years for the purposes
stated above.>r	,	n.c-.	~-v

Disapproval of this measure will prohibit the District from levying the
special tax and using the proceeds which that tax would raise.



ARGUMENT IN FAVOR OF MEASURE D r

A yes on Measure D March 7,1989, will permit the: Mojave Unified School
District to build a new middle school ^California Cityand expand the middle
school in Mojave to reduce overcrowding in the California City and Mojave
communities.	'

. Approval.of Measure D will permit the school district to collect a tax on
each parcel of property within the District for ONLY two (2) years. If
approved, the average homeowner would, pay $55,35 a year for ONLY two (2)
years. This would provide enough money to build a new school and expand a
second school without incurring interest associated with bonds. By collecting
the money before construction, property owners will SAVE approximately $8
MILLION of interest,,	-	•	:	;.1;	'

The school district is experiencing :an annual growth rate of approximately
14%, which far exceeds the state average. In the past three (3) years, 460
new children have entered district schools. Increases include::

-- A 17% enrollment increase in kindergarten through grade six in Mojave

-- A 14.9% increase in K-6 at Robert P. Ulrich Elementary School - - - •

- A 16:5% increase in 7th and 8th graders district-wide.

A yes vote on Measure D will permit the district to ;keep pace with our
soaring enrollments and make economical use of your tax dollars.
