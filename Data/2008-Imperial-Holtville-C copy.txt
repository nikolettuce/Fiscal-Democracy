IMPARTIAL ANALYSIS BY CITY ATTORNEY
HOLTVILLE UTILITY USERS TAX
MEASURE C

The City of Holtville’s Utility Users Tax was adopted by the City
Council in 1991 (Ordinance No. 415) and amended in 1993 (Ordinance
No. 422). The Utility Users Tax is a general tax devoted for general
governmental purposes including police, fire, emergency services,
administration, and parks and recreation. The Utility Users Tax is a
five percent (5%) tax on users of utilities such as defined telephone
communication services, electrical energy, gas, cable television
service, water, sewers, and garbage collection and removal. For the
fiscal year 2008-2009, anticipated general fund revenue from the tax
will approximate $527,493, almost 18 percent (18%) of the general
fund budget.

Holtville City Council has adopted resolutions to place the question
of increasing the Utility Users Tax on the November 2008 ballot. The
issue before the voters is whether or not to increase the current tax
from five percent (5%) to nine percent (9%). If approved, the four
percent (4%) increase in the Utility Users Tax must be used to fund
improvements in the Holtville Police Department, including
administration, personnel, and capital equipment. The four percent
(4%) increase could not be used for any purpose other than preserving
and maintaining Holtville’s Police Department. The tax rate cannot be
increased without approval of two-thirds or 66.66% of the voters.

Voter approval of Measure C will mean that Holtville Utility Users
Tax will increase from five percent (5%) to nine percent (9%)
commencing in January, 2009. Failure of Measure C to obtain passage
by the requisite margin will mean that the Utility Users Tax will remain
in place at the rate of five percent (5%), and additional revenues will
not be available from the Utility Users Tax to preserve and maintain
improvements within the Holtville Police Department.

Dated: 08-20-08

s/ Steven M. Walker, Attorney for City of Holtville

ANALISIS IMPARCIAL DEL ABOGADO MUNICIPAL
IMPUESTO DE USUARIOS DE SERVICIOS PUBLICOS
DE HOLTVILLE
MEDIDA C

El Impuesto sobre Usuarios de Servicios Publicos de la Ciudad de
Holtville fue adoptado por el Concejo Municipal en 1991 (Ordenanza No.
415) y modificado en 1993 (Ordenanza No. 422). El Impuesto sobre Usuarios
de Servicios Publicos es un impuesto general dedicado a propositos
gubernamentales en general incluyendo policfa, bomberos, servicios de
emergencia, administracion y parques y recreation. El Impuesto sobre
Usuarios de Servicios Publicos es un impuesto del cinco por ciento (5%)
sobre usuarios de servicios publicos tales como servicios definidos de
comunicacion telefonica, energia electrica, gas, servicios de television por
cable, agua, aguas negras, y recogimiento y elimination de la basura.
Para el primer ano fiscal 2008-2009, se anticipa que el ingreso al fondo
general del impuesto se aproximara a $527,493, casi el 18 por ciento
(18%) del presupuesto del fondo general.

El Concejo Municipal de Holtville ha adoptado una resolution para
colocar la cuestion del aumento del Impuesto sobre Usuarios de Servicios
Publicos en la boleta de noviembre, 2008. La pregunta ante los votantes
es la de aumentar o no el impuesto actual del cinco por ciento (5%) al
nueve por ciento (9%). Si es aprobado, el aumento del cuatro por ciento
(4%) en el Impuesto sobre Usuarios de Servicios Publicos debe de ser
usado para costear las mejoras del Departamento de Policia de Holtville,
incluyendo la administracion, el personal, y equipo capital. El cuatro por
ciento (4%) de aumento no podra usarse para cualquier otro proposito
excepto el de preservar y mantener el Departamento de Policia de Holtville.
La tasa de impuesto no puede ser aumentada sin la aprobacion de dos
tercios o el 66.6% de los votantes.

La aprobacion de los votantes de la Medida C va a significar que el
Impuesto sobre Usuarios de Servicios Publicos de Holtville va a aumentar
del cinco por ciento (5%) al nueve por ciento (9%) comenzando en enero,
2009. Si falla en pasar la Medida C por el margen exigido de votos esto
querra decir que el Impuesto sobre Usuarios de Servicios Publicos va a
permanecer en vigencia a razon del cinco por ciento (5%), y los ingresos
adicionales no estaran disponibles del Impuesto sobre Usuarios de
Servicios Publicos para preservar y mantener las mejoras dentro del
Departamento de Policia de Holtville.

ARGUMENT IN FAVOR OF MEASURE C

f/ Steven M. Walter, Abogado para la Ciudad de Holtville

Vote YES on Measure C to Save Holtville’s Police Department!

We rely on our city police to respond to emergencies, promote safety,
prevent crime and provide other essential services. When the city
council considered dismantling the police department and contracting
with an outside agency for police services the citizens of Holtville
spoke out overwhelmingly in favor of keeping police services under
local control because they care about crucial factors such as;
response time, familiarity with the community and the personalized
service that comes from having a police force that is based in town.

Measure C is vital to keeping our police department. The good news
for city residents is that all funding from the approval of this measure
will go directly to making the improvements necessary to save the
police department. It will allow for the hiring and retention of a chief,
and for additional officers and staff to fix the extreme staffing shortage.
It will provide for essential updates to equipment and supplies. In
addition, officers will be able to participate in requisite training and
certification programs. These resources will further improve efficiency,
productivity and response times.

A NO vote on Measure C will effectively dismantle the Holtville Police
Department and require the city to seek law enforcement services
from an outside agency at a total cost to the city budget and to the
security of the city that is not fully ascertained.

A YES vote on Measure C will allow the City of Holtville to make the
improvements necessary to keep the Holtville Police Department.

si Lisa B. Padilla
