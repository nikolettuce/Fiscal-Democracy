IMPARTIAL ANALYSIS BY CITY ATTORNEY
CITY OF CALIFORNIA CITY
MEASURE A

This measure will authorize the City ot California City to impose a special fax on
each parcel of land within the city in the amount of $100/parcel. The proceeds
from this tax levy will be place in a special account and spent only for the following
specified purposes: 30% of the proceeds shall be expended for police services.
25% of the proceeds shall be expended for fire services. 30% of the proceeds
shall be expended for street services. And 15% of the proceeds shall be ex-
pended for parks and recreation services. This tax replaces the existing special
tax (which has expired by its terms). This tax can be imposed for five years com-
mencing July 1,2007.

ARGUMENT IN FAVOR OF
MEASURE A
CITY OF CALIFORNIA CITY

More now than ever before, our City needs the special parcel tax of $100 for the
next five years. This tax replaces the $75 parcel tax that has been collected over
the last three years, has been in existence since 1998, and renewed twice. It is not
an additional tax on your property. It merely increases the amount by $25 and re-
news the term for five additional years.

Parcel tax funds have been, and will be, utilized to enhance and preserve police
and fire protection. These funds will also assist our parks and associated recre-
ational programs for our youth. Additional funds will be set aside for maintenance
of streets. For less than 28 cents per day you can make this difference in our City.
With our recent growth these funds are needed even more because, at a mini-
mum, we MUST maintain our current level of emergency services in this City. All
parcels in this City will pay the $100 tax. This means the City will get revenues
from outside the community from absentee landowners. Local residents will pay
$100 per year but get several times that in benefits. Absentee landowners will
benefit because it will make the community better and help stabilize or increase
land values. It is a great matching program that will benefit you the taxpayer and is
a positive return on your investment. Why not take advantage of this opportunity
to make the community a safer and nicer place to live and raise a family?

As your new mayor, in conjunction with the City Council, I will hold these funds as
sacred forthe purposes outlined in this measure. I believe it is the right thing to do
for our community and hope you wiil join me on March 6th and vote YES on Mea-
sure A.

s/Mayor David Evans
