Re: A RESOLUTION OF THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS OF THE STALLION
SPRINGS COMMUNITY SERVICES DISTRICT CALLING A SPECIAL
ELECTION TO CONSIDER THE APPROVAL OF THE LEVY OF A SPECIAL
TAX FOR POLICE PROTECTION SERVICES

WHEREAS, the Stallion Springs Community Services District “District” is a
Community Services District formed pursuant to the Community Services District Law; and

WHEREAS, one of the purposes of the District is to provide police protection
services for the benefit of the residents and landowners of the entire District; and

WHEREAS, the District is presently using general fund monies to fund it’s Police
Department; and

WHEREAS, Articles XIII A, Section 4, and XIII, Section 2 of the California
Constitution and the Government Codes 53978 require an election to levy a Special Tax, and

WHEREAS, the Board of Directors desire to submit for voter approval pursuant
to said provisions of the Constitution and Governmental Code section 53978 a Special Tax in the
maximum amount not to exceed $60 per parcel per year; and

NOW THEREFORE, BE IT ORDAINED BY THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS OF THE
STALLION SPRINGS COMMUNITY SERVICES DISTRICT AS FOLLOWS:

Section 1. The District has found, determined and declared that it is in the best interest of
the District, and the Community which it serves, to levy an annual special tax in the maximum
amount not to exceed $60 and to use the funds collected as a result of such levy for paying the
salaries and benefits to police protection personnel: and for other necessary police protection
expenses of the Stallion Springs Community Services District.

Section 2. The revenues collected from such special tax shall be used for the service for
which it was imposed, and for no other purpose.

Section 3. If approved by two-thirds of those voting, the special tax shall be collected in
the same manner and subject to the same penalty as, or with, taxes levied and collected by the
County of Kern.

Section 4. A special District election by mail in ballot carried out by the County Clerk in the
manner prescribed by law is hereby called by the District to be held on August 31,2004, to
approve said special tax.

Section 5. The proposition submitted to the voters shall be; “SHALL STALLION
SPRINGS COMMUNITY SERVICES DISTRICT ORDINANCE #134 LEVYING A SPECIAL
TAX IN THE AMOUNT OF $60 PER PARCEL PER YEAR FOR NECESSARY POLICE
AND MEDICAL PROTECTION EXPENSES WHICH INCLUDES POLICE SALARIES AND
OPERAf ING COSTS AND WOULD BE EFFECTIVE THE FOLLOWING FISCAL YEAR
WHEN FUNDING FOR POLICE OPERATIONS DROPS $50,000 OR MORE PER YEAR
USING FISCAL YEAR 2004 AS THE BASE, AND INCREASING THE STALLION
SPRINGS COMMUNITY SERVICES DISTRICT APPROPRIATION LIMITS SET IN
ACCORDANCE WITH ARTICLE XIII B OF THE CALIFORNIA CONSTITUTION BY THE
AUTHORIZED AMOUNT OF THE SPECIAL TAX, BE APPROVED AND BECOME
EFFECTIVE?”

The proposition shall require approval of two-thirds of the voters voting upon said
proposition.

AN ORDINANCE OF THE STALLION SPRINGS COMMUNITY SERVICES

DISTRICT, LEVYING A SPECIAL TAX FOR POLICE PROTECTION SERVICES

AND INCREASING THE STALLION SPRINGS COMMUNITY SERVICES

DISTRICT’S APPROPRIATION LIMITS BY THE AUTHORIZED AMOUNT OF

THE SPECIAL TAX.

BE IT ORDAINED by the BOARD OF DIRECTORS of the STALLION SPRINGS
COMMUNITY SERVICES DISTRICT as follows:

Section 1. Pursuant to the authority of section 4 of Article XHI-A and Section 2 of
Article XIII-C of the California Constitution and section 53978 of the Governmental Code, there
is hereby levied and assessed a special tax by the Stallion Springs Community Services District on
each parcel of property in the Community Services District for each fiscal year as needed,
commencing with the fiscal year 2006, for the purpose of funding police protection services in the
Stallion Springs Community Services District.

Section 2. The amount of said special tax for police protection services for each fiscal
year shall be $60 per parcel. In the event territory is annexed to the Stallion Springs Community
Services District following the date of adoption of this ordinance by the Board of Directors, the
special tax for police protection shall be imposed on such annexed parcels in the same amounts as
other parcels in the Community Services District and the total amount collected for the fiscal year
of such annexation and each of the fiscal years thereafter shall be increased by amounts equal to
the total special tax imposed on the annexed parcels.

Section 3. The tax imposed by the ordinance shall be a tax upon each parcel of property,
and the tax shall not be measured by the value of the property. “Parcel of property” as used in
this ordinance shall mean any contiguous unit of improved or unimproved real property held in
separate ownership, including, but not limited to, any single family residence, or any other unit or
real property.

Section 4. The special tax imposed by this ordinance shall be due in two equal
installments in accordance with the collection procedures of the Kern County Tax Collector, in
the same manner, and on the same applicable dates as established by law for the due dates for the
other charges and taxes fixed and collected by the County of Kern on behalf of the Stallion
Springs Community Services District.

Section 5. The revenue raised by this ordinance shall be placed in a special fund to be
used only for the purposes of obtaining, providing, operating and maintaining police protection

services including supplying equipment or apparatus therefore; paying the salaries and benefits to
police protection personnel; and for other necessary police protection expenses of the Stallion
Springs Community Services District.

Section 6. The Board of Directors by three (3) affirmative votes is empowered:

(A)	to establish the amount of the special tax for police protection services levied
annual each fiscal year in amounts not to exceed the maximum amount specified in Section 2 of
this ordinance as is required to provide an adequate level of police protection services in the
Community Services District in accordance with the purposes set forth on this ordinance;

(B)	To sit as a Board of Equalization under procedures to be adopted to equalize
inequities and reduce hardships created by a literal application of this ordinance, and such shall be
deemed an administrative remedy;

(C)	To annually provide an official Assessment Book designating the actual tax
levy on each parcel and to place the same on file in the office of the Community Services District

Section 7. The Board of Directors shall be empowered to amend this ordinance by three
affirmative votes of the members thereof for the purposes of carrying out the general purposes of
this ordinance in order to conform to state law that permits the County Tax Collector, or other
proper official, to collect a special tax such as is levied by this ordinance in conjunction with
County taxes or in order to assign duties pursuant to the ordinance to other officers.

Section 8. The Board of Directors is expressly prohibited from increasing the maximum
amount of the special tax levy, imposed by this ordinance absent further voter approval or as
otherwise provided by law.

Section 9. The special tax imposed hereby shall be collected in the same manner, on the
same dates, and subject to the same penalties and interest in accordance with the established
dates, as or with, other charges and taxes fixed and collected by the County of Kern on behalf of
the Stallion Springs Community Services District and the said county may deduct its reasonable
costs incurred for such services before remittal of the balance to the Stallion Springs Community
Services District.

Section 10. The special tax hereby imposed shall not be imposed upon a federal or State
governmental agency or another local agency or upon any parcel or property that is exempt from
the special tax imposed by this ordinance pursuant to any provision of the Constitution or any
paramount law.

Section 11. To the extent required by law, the appropriations limit for the Stallion Springs
Community Services District will be increased by the maximum projected aggregate collection
authorized by levy of this special tax in each of the years covered by the ordinance.

Section 12. Unexpected residue of any money raised by the Stallion Springs Community Services
District under the ordinance may only be used in the succeeding year for the purposes stated in
this ordinance, by lowering the next year’s tax by the amount unexpended, or by returning to the
taxpayers on the same pro-rata basis as originally levied.

Section 13. If any provisions of the ordinance or the application thereof to any person or
circumstances is held invalid or unconstitutional by any court of competent jurisdiction, such
invalidity or unconstitutionality shall not affect any other provisions or applications, and to this
end the provisions of this ordinance are declared to be severable. The Board of Directors and the
electorate by referendum, do hereby declare that they would have adopted this ordinance in each
section, subsection, sentence, clause, phrase, part or portion thereof, irrespective of the fact that
any one or more sections, subsections, clauses phrases, parts or portions thereof, be declared
invalid or unconstitutional.

Section 14. This ordinance, or any provisions thereof other than those provisions, if any,
which provide for modification by the Board of Directors of the Community Services District,
may only be amended or repealed by approval of two-thirds (2/3) of the voters voting on the
matter.

Section 15. This ordinance shall be referred to and shall be effective only if approved as by
two-thirds (2/3) of the voters voting at an election to be held by mail in ballot of August 31, 2004
and go into effect ten (10) days after the Kern County Clerk declares that such measure was
approved by two-thirds (2/3) of the voters voting therein.

SPRINGS COMMUNITY SERVICES DISTRICT this 17th day of February 2004.
