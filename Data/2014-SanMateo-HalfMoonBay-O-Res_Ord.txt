maintenance; and

NOW, THEREFORE, be it resolved by the City Council of the City of Half Moon Bay as follows:

Section 1. Pursuant to California Constitution Article XIIIC, Section 2(b), Government Code
Section 53724, and Elections Code Section 9222, the City Council of the City of Half Moon Bay hereby
calls and orders to be held in the City of Half Moon Bay an election of the qualified electors of said City
on November 4,2014 for the purpose of submitting to the voters an ordinance proposing the extension
of a Transactions and Use Tax, to expire after 3 additional years on April 1, 2019, as authorized by
Revenue and Taxation Code section 7285.9.

Section 2. The ordinance authorizing the Sales Tax to be approved by the voters pursuant
to Section 1 shall extend the current expiration date of the ordinance approved by Measure J in
November of 2012, and known as the City of Half Moon Bay Transactions and Use Tax Ordinance (the
and is as set forth in Exhibit A hereto. The City Council hereby approves the ordinance, the form
thereof, and its submission to the voters of the City at the November 4, 2014 election, as required by
Revenue and Taxation Code section 7285.9, subject to the approval of a majority of the voters voting on
the measure at the election. The entire text of the ordinance shall be made available to the public upon
request. The ordinance specifies that the existing tax rate one-half percent (.50%) of the sales price of
tangible personal property stored, used or otherwise consumed in the City, currently set to expire on
April 1, 2016 will be extended an additional three years, to expire April 1, 2019, unless further extended
by a majority of the voters of the City of Half Moon Bay. The State Board of Equalization shall collect the
tax from retailers subject to the tax and remit the funds to the City.

Section 6. Arguments in Favor or Against the above ballot measure extension shall be filed
with the City Clerk of the City of Half Moon Bay on or before August 15, 2014, after which no arguments
for or against the City measure may be submitted to the City Clerk.

Section 7. The Mayor or his designee(s) are hereby authorized to prepare a written
argument in favor of the proposed ordinance, not to exceed 300 words, on behalf of the City Council. At
the Mayor's discretion, the argument may also be signed by bona fide associations or by individual
voters who are eligible to vote.

Section 8. Rebuttal arguments shall be filed with the City Clerk by August 25, 2014 and
shall not exceed 250 words in length.

Section 9. The Mayor or his designee(s) are hereby authorized to prepare rebuttal
responses to any arguments filed against the ballot measure and the impartial analysis.

Section 10. The City Clerk Is hereby directed to transmit a copy of the ordinance set forth in
Section 1 above to the City Attorney, who shall prepare an impartial analysis of the measure extension
showing the effect of the measure extension on the existing law and the operation of the measure
extension in accordance with Section 9280 of the Elections Code.

Section 11. During the period commencing August 15, 2014 and August 25, 2014, the City
Clerk shall make available for public inspection the arguments filed For or Against the ballot measure
and the impartial analysis.

Section 12. The County Election Department is hereby authorized to canvass the returns of
the election. The election shall be held in all respects and if there were only one election, and only form
of ballot shall be used.

Section 13. The Board of Supervisors of the County of San Mateo is hereby requested to
issue instructions to the County Election Department to take any and all steps necessary for the holding
of the election.

ORDINANCE NO.C-2014-______

ORDINANCE AMENDING SECTION 3.10.170 "AUTOMATIC REPEAL OF TAX" OF
CHAPTER 3.10 "TRANSACTIONS AND USE TAX" OF TITLE 3 "REVENUE AND FINANCE"
OF THE HALF MOON BAY MUNICIPAL CODE EXTENDING THE TRANSACTION AND USE
TAX TO BE ADMINISTERED BY THE STATE BOARD OF EQUALIZATION AN ADDITIONAL
THREE YEARS, TO EXPIRE ON APRIL 1, 2019

THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF HALF MOON BAY DOES HEREBY ORDAIN as follows:

Section 1. Section 3.10.170 "AUTOMATIC REPEAL OF TAX" of Chapter 3.10
"TRANSACTIONS AND USE TAX" of Title 3 "REVENUE AND FINANCE" of the Half Moon
Bay Municipal Code is hereby amended to read as follows:

"3.10.170 Automatic repeal of tax.

The transactions and use tax adopted by this chapter shall be automatically
repealed and shall be of no further force and effect after March 31, 2019."

Section 2. EFFECTIVE DATE. This Ordinance relates to the levying and collecting of
the City transactions and use taxes and shall take effect immediately, but it shall not
become operative until approved by a majority of the voters voting at the general
municipal election to be held November 4, 2014.

Section 3. SEVERABILITY. If any provision of this ordinance is declared invalid by a
court of competent jurisdiction, it is the intent of the City Council that such invalid
provision be severed from the remaining provisions of the ordinance.

Section 4. PUBLICATION. The City Clerk shall cause this Ordinance to be published
in accordance with the requirements of Section 36933 of the Government Code of the
State of California.
