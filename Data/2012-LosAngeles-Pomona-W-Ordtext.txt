AN ORDINANCE OF THE CITY OF POMONA AMENDING
CHAPTER 50, ARTICLE VI OF THE POMONA CITY CODE
REGARDING THE CITY’S REAL PROPERTY TRANSFER TAX

WHEREAS, in 1967. the City of Pomona ("City") established a Real Property
Transfer Tax. which is applied at the time of sale or transfer of real property by the
County Auditor-Controller’s office and remitted to the City;

WHEREAS, in 1967, the Real Property Transfer Tax rate was SO.275 per

5500.00	valuation of the property, and in 1991, the rate was increased to Si. 10 per

5500.00	valuation;

WHEREAS, the current $1.10 tax rate has remained unchanged since 1991, or
for the last 21 years;

WHEREAS, on June 18, 2012, the City Council of the City of Pomona called a
General Municipal Election to be held on November 6, 2012, and submitted a measure to
the City’s voters, proposing an increase in the Real Property Transfer Tax from SI. 10 per
$500.00 valuation, to $2.20 per S500.00 per valuation; and

WHEREAS, although Revenue and Taxation Code Section 11911 limits the
amount of a real property transfer tax for general law cities, since the City of Pomona is a
charter city, the City of Pomona may consider additional real property transfer taxes
beyond the Section 11911 rate.

THE PEOPLE OF THE CITY OF POMONA, CALIFORNIA DO ORDAIN
AS FOLLOWS:

SECTION 1. Chapter 50, Article VI, Section 50-251 of the Pomona City Code is
hereby amended to read as follows:

Sec. 50-251. - Imposed.

There is hereby imposed on each deed, instrument or writing by which any lands,
tenements, or other realty sold within the city shall be granted, assigned,
transferred or otherwise conveyed to or vested in the purchaser or any other
person, by his direction, when the consideration or value of the interest or
property conveyed, exclusive of the value of any lien or encumbrances remaining
thereon at the time of sale, exceeds SI00.00 a tax at the rate of S2.20 §4=4=&=for
each $500.00 or fractional part thereof.

SECTION 2. Severability. If any section, subsection., sentence, clause, phrase,
or portion of this Ordinance is for any reason held to be invalid or unenforceable by a
court of competent jurisdiction, the remaining portions of this Ordinance shall
nonetheless remain in full force and effect. The people hereby declares that they would
have adopted each section, subsection, sentence, clause, phrase, or portion of this
Ordinance, irrespective of the fact that any one or more sections, subsections, sentences,
clauses, phrases, or portions of this Ordinance be declared invalid or unenforceable.

If any portion of this Ordinance is held by a court of competent jurisdiction to be
invalid, we, the voters of the City of Pomona, indicate our strong desire that: (i) the City
Council use its best efforts to sustain and cure such portion, and (ii) the City Council
implement this Ordinance by taking all steps possible to cure any inadequacies or
deficiencies identified by the court in a manner consistent with the express and implied
intent of this Ordinance.

SECTION 3. Authorization. The Mayor is hereby authorized to attest to the
adoption of this Ordinance by the voters of the City by signing where indicated below.

SECTION 4. Majority Approval; Effective Date, if a majority of voters
voting in the City’s general municipal election on November 6, 2012, approve of this
Ordinance, the Ordinance shall be considered adopted and effective upon the earliest date
legally possible after the City Clerk certifies the vote on the Ordinance by the voters of
the City of Pomona ("Effective Date"). The Mayor will sign the Ordinance, and the City
Clerk will attest and certify to the passage and adoption of this Ordinance. Upon the
Effective Date of this Ordinance, the City Manager is directed to promptly take
appropriate actions needed to implement this Ordinance.

I hereby certify that the foregoing Ordinance was PASSED, APPROVED and
ADOPTED by the people of the City of Pomona voting on the 6th day of November,
2012.
