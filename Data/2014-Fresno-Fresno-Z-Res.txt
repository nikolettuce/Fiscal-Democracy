BEFORE THE BOARD OF SUPERVISORS
OF THE COUNTY OF FRESNO
STATE OF CALIFORNIA

IN THE MATTER OF	)	Resolution	No. 14-228

INCLUSION OF BALLOT MEASURE	)

TO EXTEND MEASURE Z	)

TRANSACTIONS AND USE TAX	)

WHEREAS, on July 13, 2004, the Fresno County Board of Supervisors
established the Fresno County Zoo Authority (hereinafter the "Authority'1) pursuant to
Revenue and Taxation Code 7286.43 for the support of zoos, zoological facilities,
and related zoological purposes in Fresno County; and

WHEREAS, on November 2, 2004, voters approved an ordinance imposing a
special transactions and use tax of one tenth of one percent (0.10%) for a period of
ten (10) years, the net revenues of which are to be expended for the support of the
Fresno Chaffee Zoo; and

WHEREAS, on March 12, 2014, the Authority conducted a first reading for an
extension of the 2004 Measure Z ordinance; and

WHEREAS, on April 30, 2014, the Authority approved the ordinance after a
second reading and authorized the use of funds from its administrative budget to
fund the add-on cost to put the extension of the ordinance on the ballot; and,
WHEREAS, the Authority desires that the ordinance be placed on the
November 4, 2014 general election ballot; and,

WHEREAS, under Revenue and Taxation Code section 7286.43, the
ordinance must be approved by two-thirds of the voters of Fresno County voting on
the ordinance.

NOW, THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED AS FOLLOWS:

1.	The Fresno County Board of Supervisors approves the inclusion of an
ordinance on the November 4, 2014 Fresno County General Election ballot

to extend Measure Z for a ten-year period, and authorizes charging the
Authority the add-on cost to put the ordinance on the ballot. Said cost is

a.	The November 2014 election has been budgeted in the County

2.	The County Clerk/Elections Department shall take any and all

agrees to reimburse the County from its administrative budget for the add-on
costs of the election, and said costs shall be an obligation of the Authority
irrespective of the election outcome.

3.	The Fresno County Clerk/Registrar of Voters is designated as the
Elections Official for the election, and the Board of Supervisors hereby
authorizes and directs the Fresno County Clerk/Registrar of Voters to:

a.	Provide all notices and take all other actions necessary to holding
the election, including but not limited to providing notices of times within
which arguments for and against are submitted.

b.	Print the ordinance in its entirety in the Voter’s Information
Pamphlet section of the Sample Ballot.

4.	The ballot measure hereby approved for said election shall read as
follows:

KEEP OUR ZOO

To ensure the continuing success of Fresno Chaffee Zoo by providing
necessary funding for repairs and maintenance, improved animal care,
continuing the zoo’s Species Survival and Children’s Education Programs,
maintaining low entrance fees, improving visitor amenities, and providing
animals with safer more spacious natural habitats; shall Fresno County voters
extend the existing 1/10th of one percent sales tax for ten additional years,
with all proceeds exclusively for Fresno Chaffee Zoo?

5.	Arguments in favor of and in opposition to the ballot measure and
rebuttal arguments shall be permitted. The County hereby adopts the
provisions of Elections Code Section 9161 through 9167 regarding the
acceptance of arguments relating to ballot measures. The County Clerk shall
fix the dates for submittal of arguments and rebuttals as provided for in the
Elections Code.

6.	The County Counsel is directed to prepare an impartial analysis of the
Measure, pursuant to Elections Code Section 9160(b).

7.	The County Auditor Controller/Treasurer Tax Collector is hereby
directed to prepare a fiscal impact statement pursuant to Elections Code
section 9160(c).

8.	Upon approval of the measure by at least two-thirds of the registered
voters voting on the measure, the tax will be extended for an additional 10
years.

9.	This Resolution shall take effect on and after its adoption.
