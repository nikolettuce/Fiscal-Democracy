RESOLUTION NO. 92- 45

A RESOLUTION OF THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF LANCASTER,
CALIFORNIA, CALLING AND GIVING NOTICE OF THE HOLDING OF A
SPECIAL MUNICIPAL ELECTION TO BE HELD ON TUESDAY, JUNE 2,
1992 FOR THE ELECTION OF A PROPOSED MEASURE

WHEREAS, under the provisions of the laws relating to General Law Cities in the State
of California, a Special Election shall be held on June 2 1992 ; and

WHEREAS, the City Council desires to submit to the voters at the election a question
relating to the formation of a drainage/flood control assessment district; and

WHEREAS, the proposed measure submitted to the voters is::

Shall the City of Lancaster form a City-wide Dralnage/Flood Control Benefit

Assessment District and levy an annual assessment? YES NO

NOW. THEREFORE, THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF LANCASTER, CALIFORNIA,
DOES RESOLVE. DECLARE. DETERMINE AND ORDER AS FOLLOWS:

Section 1.	The City Council of the City of Lancaster does hereby authorize the

June 2. 1992 Special election and requests consolidation

Section 2. That the ballots to be used at the election shall be in form and content
as required by law.

Section 3. That the City Clerk Is authorized, instructed and directed to procure
and furnish any and all official ballots, notices, printed matter and all supplies, equipment
and paraphernalia that may be necessary In order to properly and lawfully conduct the
election.

Section 4. That the polls for the election shall be open at seven o'clock a.m. on the
day of the election and shall remain open continuously from that time until eight o'clock
p.m. of the same day when the polls shall be closed, except as provided In Section 14301 of the
Elections Code of the State of California.

Section 5 That In all particulars not recited In this resolution, the
election shall be held and conducted as provided by law for holding municipal elections.

Section 6 That notice of the time and place of holding the election is given and
the City Clerk Is authorized, instructed and directed to give further or additional notice of the
election, in time, form and manner as required by law.

Section 7. That the City Cleric shall certify to the passage and adoption of this
Resolution and enter it into the book of original Resolutions.

RESOLUTION NO. 92- 46

A RESOLUTION OF THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF
LANCASTER, CALIFORNIA REQUESTING THE BOARD OF
SUPERVISORS OF THE COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES TO
CONSOLIDATE A SPECIAL MUNICIPAL ELECTION TO BE
HELD ON JUNE 2. 1992 WITH THE STATEWIDE PRIMARY
ELECTION TO BE HELD ON SAID DATE, PURSUANT TO E.C. 23302.

WHEREAS, The City Council of the City of Lancaster called a Special Municipal
Election in said City to be held on Tuesday, June 2, 1992, for the purpose of submitting to the
qualified electors of the City the question :

Shall the City of Lancaster form a City-wide Drainage /Flood Control Benefit Assessment
Dlscrlct and levy an annual assessment? YES...NO...

WHEREAS, It is desirable that said Special Municipal Election be consolidated with
the statewide primary election to be held on the same date and that within said City the
precincts, polling places and election officers of the two elections be the same, and that the
Registrar of Voters of the the County of Los Angeles canvass the returns o f the Special
Municipal Election to be held In all respects as if there were only one election.

NOW, THEREFORE, THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF LANCASTER CALIFORNIA
DOES HEREBY RESOLVE AND ORDER AS FOLLOWS:

Section 1. Prpursuant to the requirement of Section 23303 of the Elections Code, the
Board of Supervisors of the County of Los Angeles is hereby requests to consent and agree to
the consolidation of a Special Municipal Election with the statewide primary election on
Tuesday, June 2, 1992 for the purpose of considering the following measure:

SHALL THE CITY OF LANCASTER FORM A CITY-WIDE DRAINAGE/FLOOD CONTROL
BENEFIT ASSESSMENT DISTRICT? YES...NO...

Section 2. Said Registrar of Voters is hereby authorized to canvass the returns of said
Special Municipal Election which it Is hereby requested to consolidate with said statewide
primary election and said election shall be held in all respects as if there were only one
election, and only one form of a ballot shall be used.

Section 3. Said Board of Supervisors is hereby requested to issue Instructions to the
Registrar of Voters to take any and all steps necessary for the holding of said consolidated
election.

Section 4. The City of Lancaster recognizes that additional costs will be Incurred by
the County by reason of this consolidation and agrees to reimburse the County for any such
costs.

RESOLUTION NO. 92-65

A RESOLUTION OF THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF
LANCASTER, CALIFORNIA, ESTABLISHING A
DRAINAGE/FLOOD CONTROL BENEFIT ASSESSMENT
DISTRICT AND AUTHORIZING THE LEVY AND
COLLECTION OF ASSESSMENTS THEREFOR

WHEREAS, the City Council of the City of Lancaster (the "City Council") has heretofore
proposed the formation of an assessment district (the "Assessment District") pursuant to the
Benefit Assessment District Act of 1982 for drainage/flood control purposes; and

WHEREAS, the proposed boundaries of the Assessment District are coterminous with the
boundaries of-the-City and are shown on a map thereof which indicates by a boundary line the
extent of the territory proposed to be included in the Assessment District, which map is designated
"Proposed Boundary Map, Drainage/Flood Control Benefit Assessment District"; and

WHEREAS, notice of the March 16,1992 pubic hearing relative to the formation of the
Assessment District to levy and collect an assessment within the boundaries of the proposed
Assessment District was published as required by the Act; and

WHEREAS, on March 16, 1992, this City Council held a noticed public hearing as
required by the Act, relative to the proposed formation of the Assessment District and the levy of
the assessment therein; and

WHEREAS, prior to the noticed public hearing there was filed with this City Council an
engineer's report (the "Report") containing a description of the service proposed to be financed
through the revenue derived from the assessments, a description of each lot or parcel of property to
be subject to the assessment, the amount of the proposed assessment, and the basis and schedule
of the assessment as required by the Act; and

WHEREAS, at the public hearing all persons desiring to be heard on all matters pertaining
to the formation of the Assessment District and the levy of the assessment were heard and a full
and fair hearing was held; and

WHEREAS, at the public hearing evidence was presented to the City Council on the
matters before it, and this City Council at the conclusion of the hearing is fully advised as to all
matters relating to the formation of the Assessment District and the levy of the assessment.

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED AND ORDERED BY THE CITY COUNCIL
OF THE CITY OF LANCASTER, STATE OF CALIFORNIA, THAT:

Section 1. Each of the above recitals is true and correct.

Section 2. A Drainage/Flood Control Benefit Assessment District to be designated
"Lancaster Drainage/Flood Control Benefit Assessment District" is hereby established pursuant to
the Act. The Board hereby finds and determines that all prior proceedings taken with respect to the
establishment of the Assessment District were valid and in conformity with the requirements of
law, including the Act.

Section 3. The diagram and assessment as set forth in the Report are hereby confirmed and
the boundaries of the Assessment District are established as shown on the Map hereinafter which

Map is on file in the office of the City Clerk and which Map, together with a notice of the
assessment, has been or shall be recorded in the County of Los Angeles Book of Maps and
Assessments and Community Facilities Districts in the Los Angeles County Recorder’s Office
pursuant to Division 4.5 (commencing with Section 3100) of the California Streets and Highways
Code.

Section 4. The Engineer is hereby directed to file the diagram and assessment, or a
certified copy thereof, with the County Auditor immediately after the adoption of this Resolution
and in no event later than the third Monday in August, 1992. After such filing, the County Auditor
shall enter on the County assessment roll opposite each lot or parcel of land the amount assessed
thereupon, as shown in the assessment.

Section 5. This City Council determines that the amounts to be assessed against the
individual parcels shown on the Map contained in the Report are hereby approved and confirmed;
and the City Clerk is hereby directed to endorse the fact and date of such approval on said Report.

Section 6. The Report, as submitted, is hereby approved and is made a part of the record
of the public hearing regarding the formation of the Assessment District, and is ordered to be kept
on file with the transcript of these proceedings and opened for public inspection.

Section 7. This Resolution shall become effective upon approval by a majority of the
registered voters voting at the election to be held on June 2,1992.

PASSED, APPROVED, AND ADOPTED this 16th day of March, 1992, by the following

vote:

Council members Rodio, Root, Theophanis, Vice Mayor Pursley and
Mayor Hearns.
